    How We Lived

    The effects of the pandemic didn't just change our economy, and it didn't just change our healthcare either. It even changed the very way we lived our lives. We saw lockdowns, restrictions, government payment packages and the rise in work from home jobs just to name a few examples. This page looks at some further examples of how the pandemic changed our way of life.

    Frequency of activities during last quarter of 2020 performed by people in Victoria aged 18 and over

    This stacked bar chart represents what activities adults in Victoria took part in for the last 3 months of 2020

    With lockdowns beginning to ease in October of 2020, Victorians started once again partaking in activities they had not enjoyed for some time. Whilst some activities, such as shopping in stores, ordering takeaway, going to parks and working from home stayed relatively the same, more close-contact actvities such as using public transport, visiting bars and attending social gatherings of more than 10 people skyrocketed in attendance once the lockdown was lifted.

    Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics, Household Impacts of COVID-19 Survey December 2020

    Percentage of stimulus payment uses for persons aged 18 years and over receiving the JobKeeper Payment

    The bar chart above provides a breakdown of how JobKeeper stimulus payments were used in 2020.

    JobKeeper allowed many businesses to stay afloat, and allowed many families to put food on their tables. The necessity of this payment is clearly shown in this chart, very few people used the stimulus for less necessary expenses such as recreation and home improvement. Certainly the vast majority of this stimulus payment's funds helped people keep a roof above their head, food on their table and power in their homes.

    Note: Respondents may have reported more than one use of the stimulus payment

    Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics, Household Impacts of COVID-19 Survey December 2020
