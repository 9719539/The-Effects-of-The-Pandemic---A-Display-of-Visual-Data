    Show Me the Money

    With radical changes to the global economic climate came global changes to expenditure. Decisions and delegations regarding money had to take many new things into account. Questions were asked, how do we keep people employed? How can those unable to work continue to feed their families? Where should economic resources be focused? Many questions arose during these times, and many changes were made. The data visualisations below aim to demonstrate two powerful examples of how the economy was impacted by the scale of the Covid 19 Pandemic.

    Percentage of change in GDP from previous year by million US$ at exchange rate

    The above line chart represents the percentage change in GDP between the years of 2018-2020 for Australia, Italy, New Zealand, Great Britain, America and China

    The pandemic was felt globally, and affected each nation differently. In this chart we see that no two GDP changes were the same, whilst some, such as Italy and Great Britain shared similarities in their trends, others experienced unique trends, like New Zealand's near-plateau, and USA's sudden drop. Interestingly, Australia experienced significant growth from 2019 to 2020, having the highest year on year increase for 2020 despite a significant decrease in 2019.

    Source: OECD Health Statistics 2020, Economic references

    Difference in Average Wage by NCU

    The above area chart set represents the changes in average wages in Australia and Italy between 2018 and 2020

    It is well known that Italy was heavily impacted by the affects of Covid-19. Whilst their economy worked to recover from the devastating impacts of the virus, the wages of the average person were not spared from economic damage. For comparison, a chart of Australia's wage growth has been included, whilst we may have struggled through lockdowns, our wages remained strong and even experienced growth.

    Source: OECD Health Statistics 2020, Economic references
