    The Effects of The Pandemic - A Display of Visual Data

    A Diagnosis of Healthcare

    Our hospitals and medical clinics faced an unprecedented wave of changes as they moved to adapt to a new challenge not seen in decades. Changes were made to how our hopsitals were run, what was prioritised, how resources were managed and even the administration of new and returning staff. As hospitals adapted and changed their approach, the data they left behind truly demonstrated just how impactful the pandemic was on our healthcare system.

    This treemap shows how some of the main resources of the Australia healthcare sector compared in numbers during the pandemic (2020)

    With hospitals almost overflowing and clinics performing seemingly endless phone consultations to unwell patients, the health sector experienced drastic pressure from the effects of Covid-19. Retired health professionals were incentivised to return to work, whilst some students were given the opportunity to receive hands-on experience by helping in the busy hospitals. It really was a unique time for healthcare, and the data shows an enormous proportion of the healthcare sector was made up by practicing nurses, real life heroes many would say are the backbone of our hospitals and bravely put themselves at risk during the pandemic to ensure people received the care they needed to deal with the immense toll of the coronavirus.

    Source: OECD Health Statistics 2020, Healthcare Resources
